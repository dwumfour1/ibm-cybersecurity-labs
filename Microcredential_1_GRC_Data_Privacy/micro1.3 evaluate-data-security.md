# Microcredential 1 — Part 3: Evaluating an Organization’s Data Security Posture (Capstone)

**Repository Context:**  
This file documents the capstone project for Microcredential 1 of the IBM SkillsBuild Cybersecurity Certificate. It demonstrates applied knowledge from the previous modules: *Data & Privacy* and *Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC)*.

---

## Project Overview

In this capstone project, I evaluated the data security posture of a fictional healthcare provider, **Cedarville Family Health**, based in Rhode Island.

The objective was to identify potential weaknesses in data security practices and assess compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). The project required applying foundational cybersecurity principles to a realistic organizational scenario.

---

## Prerequisite Knowledge Applied

This capstone builds directly on:

- Data classification and privacy principles  
- The CIA triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability)  
- Governance and administrative controls  
- Risk assessment methodologies  
- Encryption and backup strategies  

---

## Learning Objectives

After completing this project, I am able to:

1. Evaluate an organization’s data security and privacy posture  
2. Conduct a structured risk assessment  
3. Develop a practical and realistic backup strategy  
4. Recommend file and drive encryption controls  

---

## My Evaluation Approach

### 1. Data Security & Privacy Assessment
I reviewed how sensitive healthcare data (such as patient records and personal information) should be protected. Since healthcare organizations handle regulated data, maintaining confidentiality is critical.

I analyzed:
- Data handling practices  
- Access control considerations  
- Exposure risks  
- Regulatory obligations (HIPAA compliance context)

---

### 2. Risk Assessment
Using principles from the GRC module, I:
- Identified potential threats (e.g., unauthorized access, data breaches)
- Considered vulnerabilities in systems and processes
- Evaluated potential impact and likelihood
- Prioritized risks based on severity

This structured assessment allowed me to propose realistic mitigation strategies.

---

### 3. Backup Strategy Development
I designed a backup approach that:
- Protects critical healthcare data  
- Includes redundancy  
- Supports business continuity  
- Minimizes downtime in case of incident  

A strong backup plan ensures availability — one of the core pillars of the CIA triad.

---

### 4. Encryption Controls
I recommended:
- File-level encryption for sensitive documents  
- Drive-level encryption for system protection  
- Secure key management practices  

Encryption strengthens confidentiality and reduces breach impact.

Completing this project strengthened my ability to think like a security analyst — not just identifying problems, but proposing practical and compliant solutions.
