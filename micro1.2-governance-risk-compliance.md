## Key Concepts Explained

### Governance
Governance defines how security is directed and controlled within an organization.  
I learned that effective governance depends on clear:

- **Policies** (high-level rules)
- **Standards** (mandatory requirements)
- **Guidelines** (recommended best practices)
- **Procedures** (step-by-step instructions)

Strong governance ensures consistency and accountability.

---

### Risk
Risk management involves identifying and evaluating potential threats to assets.

In this module, I practiced:
- Identifying assets
- Recognizing threats and vulnerabilities
- Estimating impact and likelihood
- Prioritizing risks

Understanding risk allows organizations to allocate resources strategically rather than reacting blindly to threats.

---

### Compliance
Compliance ensures that organizations follow relevant laws, regulations, and industry standards.

I explored:
- Why compliance is legally and operationally important  
- How organizations implement administrative controls  
- The role of documentation and audits in maintaining compliance  

Compliance is not just about avoiding penalties — it strengthens trust and operational integrity.
